Brown Butter

For all flavors

Melt 3 tablespoons butter in a saucepan over medium heat and cook approx. 1-2 minutes, allowing it to bubble and foam. When the butter turns light brown and has a nutty aroma, turn the heat off immediately – do not allow it to burn.

Sage Butter

For Chanterelle / Beet / Squash

  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 2-3 fresh sage leaves

Follow recipe above for Brown Butter. When the butter has melted in the pan and before it begins to bubble, add sage leaves and cook until crisp. The crisped sage leaves can be used as a garnish for the ravioli.

Lemon Brown Butter

For Asparagus / Artichoke

  • ½ cup unsalted butter
  • Juice of 1 lemon (Meyer is best, but any lemon will do)
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • Optional: Asparagus tips, steamed

Follow recipe above for Brown Butter. Turn down heat and add the lemon juice, red pepper flakes and, if you like, asparagus tips. When ravioli are almost done, add to the sauce and continue cooking another 2 minutes.

Cumin Crème Fraîche

For Carrot & Cumin

  • ½ cup crème fraîche
  • ½ tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin

Toast cumin in a saucepan on medium heat until fragrant, about 1 minute. Add crème fraîche and lemon juice and stir to combine. Thin the mixture slightly with a few tablespoons of water. Add a pinch of salt to taste.

Horseradish Crème Fraîche

For Beef Short Rib

  • ½ cup crème fraîche
  • 2 tablespoons good creamed horseradish

In a saucepan, combine crème fraîche and creamed horseradish and melt over medium-low heat. Thin with butter or ravioli water to desired thickness.

Garlic & Tomato

For Eggplant / Taleggio & Cream / Swiss Chard / Ricotta

  • 3 red ripe tomatoes (Backyard Beauties are sold at Hannaford and work well)
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, thinly sliced

Heat olive oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add garlic and cook until golden brown, then remove from pan and set aside.

Slice tomatoes into quarters, removing the stems. Sauté until soft, then turn off the heat. Finely chop the reserved garlic and add to the pan. Salt and pepper to taste.

Caramelized Onion & Butter

For Ricotta & Parmesan

In a large saucepan, heat 1 jar good caramelized onions with 1 tablespoon butter. Thin with ravioli water as needed.

Bread Crumb & Pancetta

For Pancetta & Roasted Onion

  • ⅓ cup plus 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • ¼ pound pancetta, diced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 2 slices hearty white sandwich bread, torn into small pieces

Preheat oven to 375°. In a food processor, pulse bread into coarse crumbs (about 10 pulses – do not purée). Toss crumbs with 2 tablespoons olive oil and a dash of salt and pepper. Spread the mixture on a rimmed baking sheet and bake, stirring often, until golden, about 8 minutes.

In a large saucepan, heat remaining ⅓ cup olive oil over medium heat. Add pancetta and cook until golden, 1-2 minutes.

Turn up the heat slightly, stir in breadcrumbs, and cook for 10 seconds. Season with salt and pepper.

Tomato, Butter, & Parmigiano

For Ricotta / Grilled Eggplant / Swiss Chard

  • 5 tablespoons unsalted butter, divided
  • 1 28-oz can whole plum tomatoes, with juice – run through a food mill to remove seeds
  • 1 cup Parmigiano cheese

In a large saucepan, heat 3 tablespoons butter. Add tomatoes and simmer. Season with salt and cook about ten minutes. When ravioli are cooked, add the last 2tb butter and the cheese and stirr to combine. Add a little pasta water to thin the sauce if you like.

Gorgonzola & Walnut

For Swiss Chard

  • ½ cup shelled walnuts
  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • 3 oz sweet gorgonzola, cut into small pieces
  • 1 cup heavy cream

Heat oven to 350°. Rub a cookie sheet with a little olive oil and scatter walnuts on it. Roast about two minutes, then remove from oven, place in a food processor, and chop finely (do not purée).

In a large saucepan on medium heat, heat the butter until it begins to foam. Add walnuts and stir. Add the cream and the Gorgonzola, and salt to taste. Let cook for about 2 minutes, then turn off the heat. Thin with pasta water if you like.

Anchovies with Bread Crumbs & Pine Nuts

For Ricotta

  • 4 oz good white bread – baguette is good – cut into small chunks
  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • 4-5 oil-packed anchovy filets, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ cup pine nuts, lightly toasted
  • ½ teaspoon red pepper flakes

Preheat oven to 375°. In a food processor, pulse bread into coarse crumbs (about 10 pulses – do not purée). Toss crumbs with 2 tablespoons olive oil and a dash of salt and pepper. Spread the mixture on a rimmed baking sheet and bake, stirring often, until golden, about 8 minutes.

In a large skillet, heat remaining olive oil over medium heat. Add anchovies, garlic, and pine nuts. Then add the pepper flakes and salt, stir for 2 minutes, then turn off the heat. Add the breadcrumbs to sauce, then add the pasta.

Shallots & Cream

For Pea & Mascarpone

  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • 3 tablespoons chopped shallots
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup dry white wine
  • 1 cup heavy cream

In a large skillet over medium, heat the butter. Add the shallot and garlic and stir constantly, until soft and golden, about 3-4 minutes. Add wine and cook until reduced by half. Then add the cream, season lightly with salt and pepper, and stir until sauce begins to thicken, about 2 minutes.

*Garnish with chopped parsley and finely grated lemon zest.

Cherry Tomato

For Grilled Eggplant / Ricotta

  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 clove garlic, sliced thin
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, stems removed
  • Fresh squeezed lemon juice (optional)

Add oil and garlic to a large, unheated skillet. Cook over medium high heat until garlic begins to brown, 2-3 minutes. Add tomatoes, cook until the skins break. Don’t over cook. Transfer to a blender and purée. Add lemon juice if you like.

*Garnish with 1 cup of cherry tomatoes, halved and marinated in sea salt, cracked pepper and 1 tablespoon olive oil for 20 minutes.

Walnut Balsamico

For Squash / Tallegio & Cream

  • 6 tablespoons butter
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 2 tablespoons walnuts, coarsely chopped
  • 1 tablespoons balsamico condimento (also called balsamico glace, or balsamico reduction)
  • Parmesan and fresh Italian parsley, for garnish

In a large skillet over medium high heat, combine butter and salt and cook until the butter is just starting to bubble and brown around the edges. Add the walnuts, stir to coat, cook another minute then turn off the heat. Add the balsamico.

When the ravioli are still al dente, add to the pan with a few tablespoons of pasta water and increase the heat to high. In about a minute the ravioli will be done; add the parsley and remove from the heat. Top with grated Parmesan.

Corn Stock, Butter, & Chive

For Sweet Corn

  • 3 corn cobs
  • 1 small yellow onion
  • 6 black peppercorns
  • 1 bay leaf

  • 6 tablespoons butter
  • A bit of fresh lemon juice
  • Minced chives
  • Loose corn kernels, for garnish

To make stock, combine cobs, onion, peppercorns, bayleaf and water in a large pot. Bring to a boil then let simmer for 45 minutes. Remove solids and save the rest – this is your stock.

In a large sauce pan, combine corn stock, butter, and kernels over medium high heat and whisk together until emulsified. When the pasta is almost done cooking, remove from the pan and add to the sauce and continue cooking for another minute or so. Turn off the heat, add chives and a splash of lemon juice, and salt to taste.

Bolognese

For Ricotta

  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 3 carrots
  • 3 celery stalks
  • 1 large or 2 small onions

  • 1 pound 85% lean ground beef (do not get 90% lean beef – the fat gives the flavor)

  • 1 cup dry white wine
  • 1 cube beef broth
  • 1 cube chicken bouillon
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 can tomato paste
  • 1 large can Hunt’s diced or crushed tomatoes

  • 1 cup whole milk (no lowfat, half and half, cream, or anything else. Just do what I tell ya!)
  1. Dice the sofrito (carrots, onions, celery) and garlic.
  2. Put oil and garlic in a large heavy-bottomed pan (Le Creuset would be nice) over medium heat.
  3. After one minute, add the sofrito; sauté until soft.
  4. Add meat and brown all around but don’t burn it. Chop it up with a wooden spoon as it cooks.
  5. Add wine – cook off the alcohol for a minute, then turn heat down to a bubbling simmer.
  6. Pour in the water and beef broth. Add the chicken bouillon cube, tomato paste, and canned tomatoes. Stir to combine and let simmer for about two hours, or until the thickness you like. I get it so it coats the spoon and most of the liquid is gone.
  7. Add the milk, then simmer it down again until the desired thickness. Salt and pepper to taste.

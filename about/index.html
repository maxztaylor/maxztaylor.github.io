About A Raviolo

I started making pasta in a tiny apartment in Brooklyn. I rolled it out on our dining room table and the whole place was covered with flour by the time I was done.

Now I have the luxury of making my ravioli in a commercial kitchen, a local kid delivers farm-fresh eggs to my back door, and I can enjoy the glory of fresh Maine produce from our own garden and the Brunswick Farmer’s Market. Each raviolo is made by hand, by me, in teeny weeny batches—and yes, I still get flour everywhere.

Now that I’ve done the hard part, I hope you can relax and enjoy preparing a simple accompaniment for your ravioli. The concept is not to bury the ravioli under a heavy sauce, but to bring out the flavor of the filling and the pasta itself with a harmonious finish. The recipes here are made from basic supermarket ingredients and involve easy techniques that yield good results, even from novice cooks.

With these handmade ravioli and a quick homemade sauce, I think you’re in for a good night at the table.

Gero Apetito!

—Amy
